# Questions for Harvio Tech Industries

Use these questions to clarify expectations before the implementation scope is locked.

1. Is the expected deliverable a prototype, an MVP, or a near-production solution?
2. Should the solution be standalone, or should it integrate with an existing Harvio platform or product?
3. Which input types are mandatory in the first version: links, QR codes, documents, or all three?
4. What type of threats should be prioritized first: phishing, malware indicators, unauthorized access patterns, or all?
5. Who is the main end user of the system: school IT admin, hostel administrator, or small business operator?
6. What kind of alerts are expected: dashboard alerts, email notifications, SMS, or WhatsApp?
7. Is real-time detection expected only at the time of scanning, or should the system also support continuous monitoring?
8. Are there any restrictions on technology, deployment, hosting, or data handling?
9. What factors will matter most in evaluation: innovation, usability, accuracy, scalability, or business relevance?
10. What final deliverables are required: source code, report, presentation, prototype, or demo video?

## Suggested Message

Our team has started planning the allotted cybersecurity problem and is aiming to build a practical prototype within the expected timeline. To align our solution with Harvio's expectations, we would appreciate clarification on the deliverable type, mandatory threat formats, target users, alerting expectations, deployment constraints, and evaluation priorities.
