# Problem Statement

## Short Version

Educational institutions, hostels, and small businesses are vulnerable to cyber threats delivered through links, QR codes, and document files. Existing tools are often expensive, limited, or too complex. A low-cost, scalable system is needed to detect these threats in real time and alert administrators with clear, actionable results.

## Detailed Version

Schools, hostels, and small businesses often operate with limited cybersecurity resources, yet they frequently receive untrusted links, QR codes, and document files through email, messaging platforms, printed materials, and shared devices. These inputs can be used to deliver phishing attacks, malware payloads, credential theft attempts, or unauthorized access routes.

Most currently available security solutions either focus on a single threat format or require infrastructure, expertise, and licensing costs that are difficult for smaller organizations to maintain. As a result, many such institutions remain exposed to preventable cyber incidents that can lead to data loss, service disruption, or financial damage.

This project addresses that gap by proposing a unified system that can inspect multiple input formats, detect suspicious indicators, classify threat severity, and notify authorized administrators in a clear and usable way.
